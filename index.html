MAIN PROBLEM

Depression is a widespread and incapacitating mental health disorder that impacts millions of individuals globally. It not only diminishes daily functioning but also disrupts numerous families. Gaining insight into the diverse factors that play a role in depression is essential for effective prevention, timely intervention, and enhanced mental well-being.

OBJECTIVE

In this project, our team will employ various tests and ordered logit regression models to identify potential factors associated with depression. We will visualize the global distribution of depression to offer insights and recommendations for addressing the issue of depression. Our proposal could be seen here.

DATA SOURCE

In this project, our group would use the dataset from Kaggle which is based on DASS-42 (Kaggle).  The DASS-42 is a self-report scale consisting of 42 items intended to gauge emotional states related to depression, anxiety, and stress. According to code.txt and scoring handbook, we would divide participants into 5  depression levels for further analysis. 

Motivation 

Observing that mental health issues appear at a younger and younger age, we would like to test if the Depression, Anxiety, and Stress Scale (DASS) can potentially provide a means to objectively evaluate the severity of a patient's symptoms and the effectiveness of treatment.
